### Integration of Systems

Modern companies integrate ERP, CRM, and SCM for seamless information flow.

| Interface | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| **CRM вҶ” ERP** | Customer order from CRM triggers production in ERP |
| **SCM вҶ” ERP** | Order suggestion in SCM is booked in ERP |
| **CRM вҶ” SCM** | Delivery status from SCM in CRM for customer inquiries |

## Exam Tips

### Common Exam Questions

- What is CRM and what is it used for?
- What is SCM?
- What is the difference between ERP, CRM, and SCM?

### Important to Remember

- **CRM** = Customer relationships (sales, marketing, service)
- **SCM** = Supply chain (procurement to delivery)
- **ERP** = Internal resources (finance, HR, production)
- All three systems can be integrated

## Practice Exercises

### Exercise 1
What is CRM and which three main areas does it cover?

**Solution:** **CRM (Customer Relationship Management)** is software for managing customer relationships.

Three main areas:
1. **Sales:** Lead management, quotes, sales opportunities
2. **Marketing:** Campaigns, email marketing, customer segmentation
3. **Service:** Ticket system, complaint management, FAQ

### Exercise 2
What is the difference between ERP and CRM?

**Solution:**
| Aspect | ERP | CRM |
|--------|-----|-----|
| **Focus** | Internal business processes | Customer relationships |
| **Areas** | Finance, HR, production | Sales, marketing, service |
| **Goal** | Efficiency of resource planning | Customer satisfaction and revenue |

ERP manages internal operations, CRM focuses on the customer.

### Exercise 3
What does SCM mean and what is the goal?

**Solution:** **SCM (Supply Chain Management)** refers to the planning and control of the entire supply chain from raw material supplier to end customer.

**Goal:** Optimization of the flow of goods, information, and money along the entire supply chain for:
- Lower costs (less inventory)
- Faster delivery times
- Better availability
- Higher transparency
