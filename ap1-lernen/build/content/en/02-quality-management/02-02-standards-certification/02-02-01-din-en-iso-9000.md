# DIN EN ISO 9000ff.

## Learning Objectives

After this chapter you should be able to:
- Explain the DIN EN ISO 9000 series of standards
- Know the abbreviations DIN, EN, ISO, IEC
- Name the 7 principles of quality management
- Understand the difference between ISO 9000, 9001 and 9004

---

## Core Content

### The Standard Designation Explained

| Abbreviation | Meaning | Description |
|--------------|---------|-------------|
| **DIN** | Deutsches Institut für Normung (German Institute for Standardization) | German standards |
| **EN** | European Norm | Valid throughout Europe |
| **ISO** | International Organization for Standardization | Valid worldwide |
| **IEC** | International Electrotechnical Commission | International standards for electrical engineering |

**DIN EN ISO** means: An international ISO standard that has also been adopted as a European (EN) and German (DIN) standard.

#### The Three Main Standards in Detail

| Standard | Content | Certifiable? |
|----------|---------|--------------|
| **DIN EN ISO 9000** | Fundamentals and vocabulary of quality management | No |
| **DIN EN ISO 9001** | Requirements for a quality management system | **Yes** |
| **DIN EN ISO 9004** | Guidelines for performance improvement | No |

**Important:** Only ISO 9001 is certifiable!

### The 7 Principles of Quality Management

According to DIN EN ISO 9000, effective quality management is based on seven principles:

| No. | Principle | Description |
|-----|-----------|-------------|
| 1 | **Customer Focus** | Understanding and fulfilling customer requirements, striving to exceed customer expectations |
| 2 | **Leadership** | Leaders establish unity of purpose and direction and create conditions for achieving objectives |
| 3 | **Engagement of People** | Competent, empowered and engaged people at all levels |
| 4 | **Process Approach** | Consistent and predictable results through management of activities as interrelated processes |
| 5 | **Improvement** | Continual improvement as a permanent objective |
| 6 | **Evidence-Based Decision Making** | Decisions based on analysis and evaluation of data |
| 7 | **Relationship Management** | Managing relationships with interested parties (suppliers, partners) |

---

### Exam Trick: Recognizing False Principles

In exams, false principles are often inserted. These do **NOT** belong to the 7 principles:

| Incorrect | Why not? |
|-----------|----------|
| Striving for maximum efficiency | Too narrowly defined |
| Conducting a supplier audit | Is a method, not a principle |
| Product-oriented approach | The correct one is "Process approach" |
| Cost-based decision-making approach | The correct one is "Evidence-based decision making" |
| System-oriented management approach | Not in the current version |

### The PDCA Cycle in ISO 9001

ISO 9001 is based on the **PDCA cycle** (Plan-Do-Check-Act):

```
        ┌─────────────────┐
        │      PLAN       │
        │                 │
        └────────┬────────┘
                 │
    ┌────────────┴────────────┐
    │                         │
    ▼                         │
┌───────┐                 ┌───────┐
│  ACT  │◄────────────────│ CHECK │
│       │                 │       │
└───┬───┘                 └───┬───┘
    │                         │
    └────────────┬────────────┘
                 │
        ┌────────▼────────┐
        │       DO        │
        │                 │
        └─────────────────┘
```

## Practice Exercises

### Exercise 1
Define the following abbreviations:
- DIN
- EN
- ISO
- IEC

**Solution:**
- **DIN:** Deutsches Institut für Normung (German Institute for Standardization)
- **EN:** European Norm
- **ISO:** International Organization for Standardization
- **IEC:** International Electrotechnical Commission

### Exercise 2
Which standard of the ISO 9000 series is certifiable?

**Solution:** The DIN EN ISO 9001 (Requirements for Quality Management Systems)

### Exercise 3
Identify the correct principles of quality management:
- [ ] Customer focus
- [ ] Striving for maximum efficiency
- [ ] Process approach
- [ ] Product-oriented approach
- [ ] Continual improvement

**Solution:**
- [x] Customer focus
- [ ] Striving for maximum efficiency (incorrect)
- [x] Process approach
- [ ] Product-oriented approach (incorrect)
- [x] Continual improvement
