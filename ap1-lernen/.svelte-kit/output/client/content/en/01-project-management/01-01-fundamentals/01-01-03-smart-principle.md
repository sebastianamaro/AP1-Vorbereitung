# SMART Principle

## Learning Objectives

After this chapter, you should be able to:
- Explain the SMART principle
- Know all five letters of the acronym
- Formulate goals according to the SMART principle
- Recognize and improve non-SMART goals

---

## Core Content

### What is the SMART Principle?

The **SMART principle** is a method for formulating clear and concrete goals. SMART is an acronym that stands for five characteristics that a good goal should have.

> **NEW from 2025:** The SMART principle was newly added to the exam catalog!

---

### The Five SMART Criteria

| Letter | Meaning | Explanation |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| **S** | **Specific** | The goal is clearly and unambiguously formulated |
| **M** | **Measurable** | Success can be objectively measured |
| **A** | **Achievable** (or Accepted/Attractive) | The goal is motivating and attainable |
| **R** | **Realistic** | The goal is achievable under given conditions |
| **T** | **Time-bound** | There is a concrete time frame |

---

### Explanation of the Criteria

#### S - Specific
- Precise description of what should be achieved
- No vague formulations
- Concrete instead of general

**Not specific:** "The IT systems should be improved."
**Specific:** "The server response time should be under 200ms."

#### M - Measurable
- Quantifiable criteria
- Objectively verifiable
- Key figures defined

**Not measurable:** "Customer satisfaction should increase."
**Measurable:** "Customer satisfaction should increase from 3.5 to 4.2 points according to survey."

#### A - Achievable/Accepted
- The goal is accepted by those involved
- There is motivation to achieve the goal
- Positive formulation

**Not achievable:** "Make fewer mistakes."
**Achievable:** "Achieve an error rate of under 1%."

#### R - Realistic
- Achievable with available resources
- Neither too easy nor impossible
- Considers constraints

**Not realistic:** "Renew the entire network in one week."
**Realistic:** "Modernize the network step by step over six months."

#### T - Time-bound
- Clear end date
- Possibly intermediate dates (milestones)
- Time frame defined

**Not time-bound:** "Sometime in the future."
**Time-bound:** "By March 31, 2026."

---

### Examples of SMART Goals

#### Example 1: IT Project
**Not SMART:** "The new software should be finished soon."

**SMART:** "The new inventory management software should be completed by June 30, 2026, meet all defined requirements from the requirements document, and achieve at least 99.5% availability in test operation."

#### Example 2: Training Goal
**Not SMART:** "I want to be better at programming."

**SMART:** "By the AP1 exam on May 5, 2026, I want to be able to convert pseudocode into working Python code error-free and solve at least three practice exercises without help."

## Key Terms

| Term | Explanation |
|------|-------------|
| **SMART** | Acronym for goal formulation: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Realistic, Time-bound |
| **Goal Agreement** | Setting of goals between client and contractor |
| **Milestone** | Fixed intermediate deadline in the project |
| **Key Performance Indicator (KPI)** | Metric for verifying goal achievement |

## Exam Tips

### Typical Exam Question

> "A project goal should be developed according to the SMART rule. Which of the following characteristics must the goal have?"
>
> [ ] specific [ ] spontaneous [ ] metric
> [ ] special [ ] measurable [ ] temporary
> [ ] mediocre [ ] redundant [ ] accepted
> [ ] realistic [ ] time-bound [ ] traditional

**Solution:** specific, measurable, accepted (or achievable), realistic, time-bound

### Additional Tips
- Know all five letters by heart
- Know alternative designations (A = Achievable OR Accepted OR Attractive)
- Be able to distinguish examples of SMART and non-SMART goals

## Practice Exercises

### Exercise 1
What does the acronym SMART stand for?

**Solution:**
- S = Specific
- M = Measurable
- A = Achievable (or Accepted)
- R = Realistic
- T = Time-bound

### Exercise 2
Reformulate the following goal to be SMART:
"The website should be faster."

**Possible Solution:**
"The loading time of the homepage should be reduced from currently 4 seconds to under 2 seconds by December 31, 2025."

### Exercise 3
Which SMART criteria are missing from the following goal?
"Customer satisfaction should be improved."

**Solution:**
- **M missing:** Not measurable (by how much?)
- **T missing:** Not time-bound (by when?)
- Possibly also **S missing:** Not specific enough (which aspect of satisfaction?)
