# Data Subject Rights

## Learning Objectives

After this chapter, you should be able to:
- Know the most important data subject rights under GDPR
- Explain the individual rights
- Know how data subjects can exercise their rights
- Know the consequences of violations

---

## Core Content

### What are Data Subject Rights?

The GDPR grants **data subjects** comprehensive rights to ensure control over their personal data. These rights are regulated in **Articles 12-22 GDPR**.

### Individual Rights in Detail

#### 1. Right to Information (Art. 13/14 GDPR)

Data subjects must be informed when their data is collected about:
- Identity of the controller
- Purposes of processing
- Legal basis
- Recipients of the data
- Storage period
- Data subject rights

**Implementation:** Privacy policy on websites, information sheets

---

#### 2. Right of Access (Art. 15 GDPR)

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **What?** | Data subjects can request a copy of their stored data |
| **Scope** | Which data, processing purposes, recipients, storage period |
| **Deadline** | Response within one month |
| **Costs** | First copy free of charge |

---

#### 3. Right to Rectification (Art. 16 GDPR)

- Inaccurate data must be **corrected**
- Incomplete data must be **supplemented**
- Upon request by the data subject

---

#### 4. Right to Erasure - "Right to be Forgotten" (Art. 17 GDPR)

Erasure can be requested when:
| Reason | Example |
|--------|---------|
| Data no longer needed | Purpose fulfilled |
| Consent withdrawn | Newsletter unsubscribed |
| Unlawful processing | Without legal basis |
| Objection filed | No overriding grounds |

**Exceptions:** Legal retention requirements, legal claims

---

#### 5. Right to Restriction of Processing (Art. 18 GDPR)

- Data is **blocked** but not deleted
- Application e.g., during verification of accuracy
- Data may only be stored

---

#### 6. Right to Data Portability (Art. 20 GDPR)

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **What?** | Receive data in machine-readable format |
| **Or** | Direct transfer to another provider |
| **Prerequisite** | Automated processing, consent or contract |
| **Example** | Switching to another cloud provider |

---

#### 7. Right to Object (Art. 21 GDPR)

- Processing can be objected to on **personal grounds**
- For direct marketing: **At any time** without justification
- Controller must demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds

---

#### 8. Right Against Automated Individual Decisions (Art. 22 GDPR)

- No legally significant decision **solely by algorithms**
- Example: Automatic credit rejection
- Exceptions: Contract performance, consent, legal authorization
- In case of exception: Right to review by a person

---

### Deadlines and Implementation

| Right | Response Deadline |
|-------|-------------------|
| Access | 1 month (extendable to 3) |
| Rectification | Without undue delay |
| Erasure | Without undue delay |
| All others | 1 month |

---

### Consequences of Violations

| Violation | Consequence |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Fines** | Up to 20 million EUR or 4% of annual turnover |
| **Complaint** | Can be filed with supervisory authority |
| **Damages** | Data subjects can claim compensation |

---

## Key Terms

| Term | Explanation |
|------|-------------|
| **Data Subject** | Person whose data is being processed |
| **Right of Access** | Right to a copy of own data |
| **Right to be Forgotten** | Right to have data erased |
| **Data Portability** | Right to data in machine-readable format |
| **Right to Object** | Right to prohibit processing |

## Exam Tips

### Common Exam Questions
- Name three data subject rights under GDPR
- What is the "right to be forgotten"?
- What does data portability mean?

### Important to Remember
- **Access** = Copy of own data
- **Erasure** = "To be forgotten"
- **Data Portability** = Machine-readable format
- Deadline usually **1 month**

## Practice Exercises

### Exercise 1
Name three rights of data subjects under GDPR.

**Solution:**
1. Right of access (Art. 15)
2. Right to erasure (Art. 17)
3. Right to rectification (Art. 16)
(or: Data portability, Object, Restriction)

### Exercise 2
A customer wants to know what data an online shop has stored about them. Which right can they invoke?

**Solution:** The **right of access** under Art. 15 GDPR. The customer can request a copy of all stored data. The online shop must respond within one month.

### Exercise 3
What does "right to be forgotten" (Art. 17 GDPR) mean?

**Solution:** The right to be forgotten is the right to erasure of personal data. Data subjects can request that their data be deleted if, for example, the processing purpose has ceased, consent has been withdrawn, or processing was unlawful.
