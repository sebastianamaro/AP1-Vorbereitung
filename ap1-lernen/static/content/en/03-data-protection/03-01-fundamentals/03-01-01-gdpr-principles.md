# GDPR Principles

## Learning Objectives

After this chapter, you should be able to:
- Classify the GDPR as an EU-wide regulation
- Know the 6 principles of data processing
- Name the legal bases for data processing
- Understand the relationship between GDPR and BDSG

---

## Core Content

### What is the GDPR?

The **General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)** is an EU regulation that has been in force since May 25, 2018. It uniformly regulates the protection of personal data throughout the EU.

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Full Name** | Regulation (EU) 2016/679 |
| **Entry into Force** | May 25, 2018 |
| **Scope** | All EU member states |
| **Objective** | Protection of personal data of natural persons |

---

### GDPR and BDSG

| Law | Function |
|-----|----------|
| **GDPR** | EU-wide basic regulation |
| **BDSG** | Supplements GDPR for Germany (uses "opening clauses") |

The **Federal Data Protection Act (BDSG)** supplements the GDPR with national regulations, e.g.:
- Regulations on the Data Protection Officer (Section 38 BDSG)
- Employee data protection (Section 26 BDSG)
- Video surveillance (Section 4 BDSG)

#### The Principles in Detail

| No. | Principle | Article | Meaning | Example |
|-----|-----------|---------|---------|---------|
| 1 | **Lawfulness, Transparency** | Art. 5(1)(a) | Processing only with legal basis, comprehensible to data subjects | Privacy policy on website |
| 2 | **Purpose Limitation** | Art. 5(1)(b) | Collect data only for specified, legitimate purposes | No use of customer data for advertising without consent |
| 3 | **Data Minimization** | Art. 5(1)(c) | Collect only necessary data | No date of birth for newsletter registration |
| 4 | **Accuracy** | Art. 5(1)(d) | Data must be factually correct and up-to-date | Update address changes promptly |
| 5 | **Storage Limitation** | Art. 5(1)(e) | Store data only as long as necessary | Delete applicant data after rejection |
| 6 | **Integrity and Confidentiality** | Art. 5(1)(f) | Appropriate security through technical and organizational measures | Encryption, access controls |

---

### Legal Bases for Data Processing (Art. 6 GDPR)

Personal data may only be processed if a **legal basis** exists:

| Legal Basis | Explanation |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Consent** (Art. 6(1)(a)) | Data subject has agreed |
| **Contract** (Art. 6(1)(b)) | Necessary for contract performance |
| **Legal Obligation** (Art. 6(1)(c)) | Required by law |
| **Vital Interests** (Art. 6(1)(d)) | Protection of vital interests |
| **Public Interest** (Art. 6(1)(e)) | In the public interest |
| **Legitimate Interest** (Art. 6(1)(f)) | Prevailing legitimate interest |

---

### Requirements for Consent (Art. 7 GDPR)

Valid consent must be:

| Requirement | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| **Freely Given** | No disadvantages if refused |
| **Informed** | Clear information about purpose and scope |
| **Unambiguous** | Active agreement (opt-in), no pre-selected checkboxes |
| **Withdrawable** | Can be withdrawn at any time without disadvantages |

## Key Terms

| Term | Explanation |
|------|-------------|
| **GDPR** | General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 |
| **BDSG** | Federal Data Protection Act (Germany) |
| **Opening Clause** | Allows national additions to the GDPR |
| **Legal Basis** | Legal permission for data processing |
| **Consent** | Voluntary agreement to data processing |
| **Purpose Limitation** | Use data only for specified purpose |
| **Data Minimization** | Collect only necessary data |

## Practice Exercises

### Exercise 1
What does the GDPR regulate and what does the BDSG regulate?

**Solution:**
- **GDPR:** Regulates data protection uniformly throughout the EU (regulation)
- **BDSG:** Supplements the GDPR with national German regulations (uses opening clauses)

### Exercise 2
Name three principles of data processing according to Art. 5 GDPR.

**Solution:**
1. Lawfulness and transparency
2. Purpose limitation
3. Data minimization
(or: Accuracy, Storage limitation, Integrity and Confidentiality)

### Exercise 3
An online shop wants to use customer data for newsletter advertising. Which legal basis could apply?

**Solution:** **Consent** (Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR) is the appropriate legal basis. The customer must actively agree that their data may be used for newsletter advertising.
