## Core Content

### What is Certification?

**Certification** is formal proof that an organization, a product, or a person meets established standards. It is carried out by independent, external testing bodies.

**Benefits of certification:**
- Proof of quality capability to customers
- Competitive advantage in the market
- Systematic improvement of internal processes
- Fulfillment of customer requirements (e.g., for tenders)
- Building trust with business partners

---

### The Audit

An **audit** is a systematic, documented examination procedure for assessing compliance with standards.

#### Types of Audits

| Audit Type | Description | Conducted by |
|------------|-------------|--------------|
| **Internal Audit** | Self-examination by own employees | Internal to the organization |
| **External Audit** | Examination by independent body | Certification body |
| **Supplier Audit** | Examination of suppliers | Customer examines supplier |

#### The Auditor

An **auditor** is a specially trained person who conducts audits:

- Independent and objective
- Technically qualified
- For external audits: from an accredited body

#### The Phases in Detail

| Phase | Activities |
|-------|------------|
| **1. Preparation** | Build QMS, create documentation, conduct internal audits |
| **2. Application** | Select certification body (e.g., TUV, DEKRA), submit application |
| **3. Audit** | Stage 1: Document review; Stage 2: On-site audit |
| **4. Certificate** | If positive result: certificate (typically valid for 3 years) |
| **5. Surveillance** | Regular surveillance audits (annually), re-certification after 3 years |

---

### Accreditation vs. Certification

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Certification** | Confirmation that an organization/product meets standards |
| **Accreditation** | Confirmation that a certification body is competent to perform certifications |

**Remember:** An accredited body certifies organizations.

## Key Terms

| Term | Explanation |
|------|-------------|
| **Certification** | Formal proof of meeting standards |
| **Audit** | Systematic examination procedure |
| **Auditor** | Specially trained examiner |
| **Certification Body** | Organization that performs certifications |
| **Accreditation** | Recognition of a certification body |
| **Surveillance Audit** | Regular examination to maintain the certificate |
| **Re-certification** | Complete new certification after expiry |

## Practice Exercises

### Exercise 1
What is certification and what benefit does it have for an organization?

**Solution:** Certification is formal proof that an organization meets established standards. Benefits:
- Proof of quality capability
- Competitive advantage
- Building trust with customers and partners
- Prerequisite for certain contracts/tenders

### Exercise 2
Describe the certification process in five steps.

**Solution:**
1. **Preparation:** Self-evaluation, documentation, internal audits
2. **Application:** Choose certification body, submit application
3. **Audit:** Document review and on-site audit by external auditors
4. **Certificate:** Issuance of certificate upon positive result
5. **Surveillance:** Regular surveillance audits to maintain certification

### Exercise 3
Distinguish between internal and external audits.

**Solution:**
- **Internal audit:** Self-examination by own trained employees for preparation and improvement
- **External audit:** Examination by independent certification body for official certification
