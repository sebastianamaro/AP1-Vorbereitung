# Offer Comparison

## Learning Objectives

After this section, you will be able to:
- Apply quantitative and qualitative comparison methods
- Calculate the purchase price (landed cost)
- Systematically evaluate offers
- Determine the best procurement alternative

### Purchase Price Calculation

The **purchase price** (also called landed cost or acquisition cost) is the actual price a company pays for goods - including all deductions and additions.

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                    PURCHASE PRICE FORMULA                            │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│                                                                      │
│      List purchase price (net)                                      │
│    - Supplier discount                                              │
│    ─────────────────────────────                                    │
│    = Target purchase price                                          │
│    - Supplier cash discount                                         │
│    ─────────────────────────────                                    │
│    = Net purchase price                                             │
│    + Procurement costs                                              │
│      (Packaging, transport, insurance, customs)                     │
│    ─────────────────────────────                                    │
│    = PURCHASE PRICE (Landed cost)                                   │
│                                                                      │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Example Calculation

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│          PURCHASE PRICE CALCULATION - EXAMPLE                        │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│                                                                      │
│  ConSystem GmbH orders 10 laptops:                                  │
│                                                                      │
│  List purchase price (net):    $1,000.00 x 10 = $10,000.00         │
│  - Volume discount 15%:                       -  $1,500.00          │
│  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────      │
│  = Target purchase price:                        $8,500.00          │
│  - Cash discount 2% (payment within 10 days):  -   $170.00          │
│  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────      │
│  = Net purchase price:                           $8,330.00          │
│  + Shipping costs:                             +   $150.00          │
│  + Packaging:                                  +    $50.00          │
│  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────      │
│  = PURCHASE PRICE:                               $8,530.00          │
│                                                                      │
│  Purchase price per laptop: $8,530 / 10 = $853.00                  │
│                                                                      │
│  Comparison:                                                        │
│  List price:    $1,000 per laptop                                   │
│  Purchase price:  $853 per laptop                                   │
│  Savings:         $147 per laptop (14.7%)                           │
│                                                                      │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Payment Terms Terminology

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                    PAYMENT TERMS                                     │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│                                                                      │
│  DISCOUNT                                                           │
│  → Price reduction on purchase                                      │
│  → E.g., volume discount, loyalty discount, special discount        │
│  → Deducted from list price                                         │
│                                                                      │
│  CASH DISCOUNT (SKONTO)                                             │
│  → Price reduction for quick payment                                │
│  → E.g., "2% cash discount for payment within 10 days"              │
│  → Deducted from target purchase price                              │
│                                                                      │
│  PAYMENT TERM                                                       │
│  → Period until payment is due                                      │
│  → E.g., "Net 30" = payment within 30 days without deduction        │
│                                                                      │
│  Typical wording:                                                   │
│  "2% cash discount for payment within 10 days, net 30 days"         │
│  → Within 10 days: 2% cash discount                                 │
│  → Within 30 days: No cash discount, but no late fee               │
│  → After 30 days: Payment is overdue                                │
│                                                                      │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Qualitative Comparison

| Criterion | Supplier A | Supplier B | Supplier C |
|-----------|------------|------------|------------|
| Purchase price | $8,530 | $8,443.50 | $8,400 |
| Delivery time | 3 days | 7 days | 14 days |
| Warranty | 3 years | 2 years | 5 years |
| Support | Phone + On-site | Phone only | Phone + Chat |
| Return policy | 14 days | 30 days | 14 days |

**Conclusion:** The cheapest supplier (C) has the longest delivery time. The decision depends on priorities!

## Key Terms

| Term | Explanation |
|------|-------------|
| **List purchase price** | Original price without deductions |
| **Discount** | Price reduction (e.g., volume discount) |
| **Target purchase price** | List price after discount |
| **Cash discount** | Price reduction for quick payment |
| **Net purchase price** | Price after cash discount |
| **Procurement costs** | Transport, packaging, insurance |
| **Purchase price** | Actual acquisition cost (landed cost) |
| **Payment term** | Period until payment is due |

---

## Exam Tips

1. **Learn the formula by heart:**
   - List price - Discount = Target purchase price
   - Target purchase price - Cash discount = Net purchase price
   - Net purchase price + Procurement costs = **Purchase price**

2. **Note the order:**
   - First discount, then cash discount!
   - Cash discount is calculated from TARGET PURCHASE PRICE (not list price!)

3. **Typical exam mistakes:**
   - Calculating cash discount from list price (WRONG!)
   - Forgetting procurement costs
   - Wrong percentage calculation (15% of 1000 = 150, not 850!)

4. **Exam format:**
   - Often: Fill in table with blanks
   - Or: Determine the cheapest supplier

## Practice Exercises

### Exercise 1: Purchase Price Calculation

ConSystem GmbH receives the following offer for 20 monitors:

- List price: $300 per unit
- Volume discount: 10%
- Cash discount: 3% for payment within 14 days
- Delivery costs: $80 flat
- Packaging: $2 per unit

Calculate the purchase price and unit price.

<details>
<summary>Show Solution</summary>

**Calculation:**

List purchase price: $300 x 20 = **$6,000.00**
- Discount 10%: **- $600.00**
───────────────────────────────────────
= Target purchase price: **$5,400.00**
- Cash discount 3%: **- $162.00**
───────────────────────────────────────
= Net purchase price: **$5,238.00**
+ Delivery costs: **+ $80.00**
+ Packaging (20 x $2): **+ $40.00**
───────────────────────────────────────
= **Purchase price: $5,358.00**

**Unit price:** $5,358 / 20 = **$267.90**

</details>

### Exercise 2: Offer Comparison

Compare two offers for 50 keyboards:

| Item | Supplier X | Supplier Y |
|------|------------|------------|
| List price/unit | $45 | $42 |
| Discount | 20% | 15% |
| Cash discount | 2% | 3% |
| Shipping costs | $50 | free |

Which supplier is cheaper?

<details>
<summary>Show Solution</summary>

**Supplier X:**
- List price: $45 x 50 = $2,250
- Discount 20%: - $450
- Target purchase price: $1,800
- Cash discount 2%: - $36
- Net purchase price: $1,764
- Shipping: + $50
- **Purchase price X: $1,814**

**Supplier Y:**
- List price: $42 x 50 = $2,100
- Discount 15%: - $315
- Target purchase price: $1,785
- Cash discount 3%: - $53.55
- Net purchase price: $1,731.45
- Shipping: $0
- **Purchase price Y: $1,731.45**

**Result: Supplier Y is $82.55 cheaper.**

(Although the discount is lower, the higher cash discount and free shipping make the difference.)

</details>

### Exercise 3: Combined Evaluation

ConSystem GmbH has received the following offers for servers:

| Criterion | Supplier 1 | Supplier 2 |
|-----------|------------|------------|
| Purchase price | $12,500 | $11,800 |
| Delivery time | 5 days | 21 days |
| Warranty | 5 years | 3 years |
| Support | 24/7 On-site | Phone weekdays |

The server is urgently needed (project starts in 10 days). In case of failure, high costs arise.

a) Which supplier is quantitatively cheaper?
b) What qualitative factors support Supplier 1?
c) What is your recommendation?

<details>
<summary>Show Solution</summary>

a) **Quantitatively:** Supplier 2 is $700 cheaper ($11,800 vs. $12,500)

b) **Qualitative advantages of Supplier 1:**
- Delivery time 5 days (project can start!)
- 5-year warranty (2 years longer)
- 24/7 on-site support (critical for server failure)

c) **Recommendation: Supplier 1**

Reasoning:
- Supplier 2 cannot deliver on time (21 days > 10 days until project start)
- The additional cost of $700 is small compared to project costs from delay
- Better support reduces downtime risks
- Longer warranty saves costs long-term

The lower price of Supplier 2 cannot outweigh the disadvantages.

</details>
