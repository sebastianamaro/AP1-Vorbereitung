### Comparison: Direct vs. Indirect

| Aspect | Direct Sales | Indirect Sales |
|--------|--------------|----------------|
| **Intermediaries** | None | Wholesaler/Retailer |
| **Margin** | Higher | Lower (dealer margin) |
| **Customer contact** | Direct | Through dealer |
| **Reach** | Limited | Large |
| **Investment** | High (own sales force) | Lower |
| **Control** | High | Limited |

### Distribution Channels in IT

| Distribution Channel | Description | IT Example |
|---------------------|-------------|------------|
| **Online Shop** | Direct sales via internet | Dell.com, apple.com |
| **System Integrator** | Consulting + Sales + Service | Local IT service providers |
| **Specialty Retail** | Specialized dealer | Computer store |

| **Reseller** | Authorized reseller | Software license dealers |
| **Wholesaler** | Sales to dealers | Also, Ingram Micro |

## Key Terms

| Term | Explanation |
|------|-------------|
| **Direct sales** | Sales without intermediaries |
| **Indirect sales** | Sales through dealers |
| **B2B** | Business-to-Business (companies to companies) |
| **B2C** | Business-to-Consumer (companies to end customers) |
| **C2C** | Consumer-to-Consumer (private to private) |
| **E-Commerce** | Electronic commerce via internet |
| **Omnichannel** | Integration of multiple sales channels |
| **Reseller** | Authorized reseller |
| **SaaS** | Software as a Service (subscription model) |

## Exam Tips

1. **Remember abbreviations:**
   - B2B = **B**usiness to **B**usiness
   - B2C = **B**usiness to **C**onsumer
   - C2C = **C**onsumer to **C**onsumer

2. **Typical exam questions:**
   - Which distribution channel for IT services?
   - Advantages/disadvantages of direct sales
   - Assign e-commerce models

3. **Important for IT:**
   - System integrator = Indirect sales with added value (consulting)
   - SaaS = Modern software distribution

## Practice Exercises

### Exercise 1: E-Commerce Models

Assign the following examples to the correct e-commerce model:

a) A software manufacturer sells licenses to companies
b) A private seller offers their used PC on eBay
c) An online shop sells electronics to end consumers
d) A government agency procures new computers through a tender

<details>
<summary>Show Solution</summary>

a) **B2B** (Business-to-Business) - Company sells to companies
b) **C2C** (Consumer-to-Consumer) - Private to private
c) **B2C** (Business-to-Consumer) - Company to end consumers
d) **B2G** (Business-to-Government) - Company to public sector

</details>

### Exercise 2: Distribution Channel Decision

ConSystem GmbH is a mid-sized IT system integrator and wants to optimize its sales. Currently, customers are served exclusively through field sales.

a) Name two advantages of the current direct sales approach.
b) What additional sales channels could be useful?
c) What does "omnichannel" mean in this context?

<details>
<summary>Show Solution</summary>

a) **Advantages of direct sales:**
- Direct customer contact and personal consulting
- Higher margin (no dealer commission)
- Control over the customer experience
- Direct feedback from customers

b) **Additional sales channels:**
- Online shop for standard products (hardware, licenses)
- Partner program with other service providers
- Webinars and online consulting
- Social media for lead generation
- Cooperation with wholesalers

c) **Omnichannel:**
Integration of all sales channels into a unified customer experience. The customer can:
- Research online
- Receive phone consultation
- Close the contract through field sales
- Get support through all channels
- All information is connected

</details>

### Exercise 3: Software Distribution Models

Explain the difference between the following software distribution models:

a) License purchase (classic)
b) SaaS (Software as a Service)
c) Freemium

<details>
<summary>Show Solution</summary>

a) **License purchase (classic):**
- One-time payment for permanent use
- Software is installed on own systems
- Updates often cost extra
- Example: Microsoft Office 2021 (purchase version)

b) **SaaS (Software as a Service):**
- Regular payment (monthly/yearly)
- Software runs in the cloud
- Updates automatically included
- Example: Microsoft 365, Salesforce

c) **Freemium:**
- Basic version free
- Premium features paid
- Goal: Convert users to paid version
- Example: Dropbox (2 GB free), Spotify

</details>
