# Total Quality Management (TQM)

## Learning Objectives

After this chapter you should be able to:
- Explain TQM as a management philosophy
- Know the core principles of TQM
- Describe the EFQM model
- Distinguish TQM from ISO 9001

---

## Core Content

### What is TQM?

**Total Quality Management (TQM)** is a comprehensive management philosophy that was developed in the USA in the 1940s and successfully implemented in Japan in the 1950s.

**The three components of the name:**

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Total** | All areas and all employees are involved |
| **Quality** | Quality is at the center of all activities |
| **Management** | Systematic leadership and control |

#### The Five Pillars of TQM

| Principle | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Customer Focus** | The customer is at the center; Quality = fulfilling customer expectations |
| **Employee Involvement** | All employees are responsible for quality and are actively involved |
| **Process Orientation** | Focus on processes rather than departments |
| **Continuous Improvement (CIP)** | Constant striving for improvement (Kaizen) |
| **Leadership by Example** | Management exemplifies quality awareness |

### TQM vs. ISO 9001

| Aspect | TQM | ISO 9001 |
|--------|-----|----------|
| **Type** | Philosophy/Concept | Standardized requirements |
| **Goal** | Excellence, continuous improvement | Meet minimum requirements |
| **Certification** | No formal certification | Certifiable |
| **Focus** | Corporate culture | Processes and documentation |
| **Employees** | Cultural change required | Regulatory compliance |
| **Assessment** | Self-assessment (EFQM) | External audit |

---

### Continuous Improvement Process (CIP)

**CIP** is a central element of TQM:

- **Kaizen** (Japanese): "Change for the better"
- Small, continuous improvements instead of major upheavals
- Every employee contributes to improvements
- Problems are seen as opportunities for improvement

**CIP Tools:**
- Improvement suggestions
- Quality circles
- PDCA cycle
- Cause-and-effect analysis

## Exam Tips

### Common Exam Questions
- What is TQM?
- Name the core principles of TQM
- What is the difference between TQM and ISO 9001?

### Important to Remember
- TQM is a **philosophy**, not a standard
- **All employees** are involved
- **Continuous improvement** is central
- TQM is **not certifiable**, EFQM offers self-assessment

## Practice Exercises

### Exercise 1
Explain what the three letters in TQM stand for.

**Solution:**
- **T (Total):** All areas and all employees of the organization are involved
- **Q (Quality):** Quality is at the center of all activities
- **M (Management):** Systematic leadership and control of quality efforts

### Exercise 2
Name three core principles of TQM.

**Solution:**
1. Customer focus
2. Employee involvement
3. Continuous Improvement (CIP)
(or: Process orientation, Leadership by example)

### Exercise 3
What distinguishes TQM from ISO 9001?

**Solution:**
- TQM is a **philosophy**, ISO 9001 is a **standard**
- TQM strives for **excellence**, ISO 9001 defines **minimum requirements**
- TQM is **not certifiable**, ISO 9001 is **certifiable**
- TQM requires a **cultural change**, ISO 9001 requires **regulatory compliance**
