# IHK Final Examination - Exam Structure

## Learning Objectives

After this chapter, you should be able to:
- Understand the structure of the extended IHK final examination
- Know the weighting of individual exam components
- Place the exam schedule in context
- Know the five action steps of Part 1

---

## Core Content

### Overview: The Extended Final Examination

With the reorganization of IT professions from August 2020, an **extended IHK final examination** was introduced. The examination is divided into two parts:

| Exam Part | Timing | Weighting |
|-----------|--------|-----------|
| **Part 1 (AP1)** | 18 months after training begins | **20%** |
| **Part 2 (AP2)** | At the end of training | **80%** |

---

### Part 1: Setting Up an IT-Supported Workplace

#### Key Facts

- **Duration:** 90 minutes
- **Format:** Practice-oriented written examination
- **Weighting:** 20% of total grade
- **Validity:** Same for all IT professions
- **Tasks:** 4 open-ended (unstructured) tasks
- **Points:** 100 points total (20-30 points per task)

#### Affected Training Professions

AP1 is identical for:
- IT Specialist (all specializations)
- IT System Electronics Technician
- IT System Management Clerk
- Digitalization Management Clerk

#### The Five Action Steps (Competency Areas)

Exam tasks are oriented around five action steps:

1. **Determine Customer Needs in a Target Group-Oriented Manner**
   - Conduct needs analysis
   - Distinguish target groups
   - Document requirements

2. **Select Hardware and Software and Initiate Procurement**
   - Evaluate market-standard IT systems
   - Obtain and compare offers
   - Assess cost-effectiveness

3. **Set Up IT Workplaces**
   - Configure and test systems
   - Consider accessibility
   - Create documentation

4. **Monitor and Document Service Delivery**
   - Conduct quality assurance
   - Create acceptance protocols
   - Record and evaluate costs

5. **Implement IT Security and Data Protection**
   - Analyze security requirements
   - Derive and implement measures
   - Comply with legal requirements

---

### Part 2: Further Examinations

Part 2 consists of several components (varies by specialization):

| Component | Duration | Weighting |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| Operational Project Work + Presentation + Technical Discussion | 40-80 hrs + 30 min | **50%** |
| Profession-Related Written Exam 1 | 90 minutes | **10%** |
| Profession-Related Written Exam 2 | 90 minutes | **10%** |
| Economics and Social Studies | 60 minutes | **10%** |

---

### Exam Dates

#### Summer Examination
- **Part 1 (AP1):** Early May
- **Part 2 written:** June/July
- **Project proposal:** January

#### Winter Examination
- **Part 1 (AP1):** Late November
- **Part 2 written:** Mid-January
- **Project proposal:** August/September

> **Note:** The written exam dates are uniform nationwide. Other dates may vary slightly depending on the IHK.

---

### Passing Requirements

To pass the exam, the following conditions must be met:

- At least **"satisfactory"** in the overall result (Part 1 + Part 2)
- At least **"satisfactory"** in the Part 2 result
- At least **"satisfactory"** in at least **three exam areas** of Part 2
- **No exam area** of Part 2 with "inadequate"

#### Oral Supplementary Examination

If necessary, a 15-minute oral supplementary examination can be requested in certain areas. The result is weighted 2:1 (written:oral).

---

## Key Terms

| Term | Explanation |
|------|-------------|
| **Extended Final Examination** | Exam format where the final examination is divided into two temporally separated parts |
| **AP1** | Final Examination Part 1 - the first exam after 18 months |
| **AP2** | Final Examination Part 2 - the exams at the end of training |
| **Open-ended Tasks** | Open questions without predetermined answer options |
| **Action Steps** | The five competency areas tested in AP1 |

---

## Exam Tips

### Before the Exam
- Start preparation early
- Practice all five action steps equally
- Work through exam simulations

### During the Exam
1. **First 6-8 minutes:** Read through all tasks and determine the order
2. **Task selection:** Prioritize by achievable points, not by preference for the topic
3. **Write something for all sub-tasks:** Even attempts can earn partial credit
4. **For unclear task descriptions:** Briefly describe your own interpretation and explain your approach - this way partial or even full points can be awarded
5. **Work efficiently:** No perfectionism in design, focus on content
