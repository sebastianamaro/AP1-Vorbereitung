# The Projektdokumentation (Project Documentation)

## Learning Objectives

After this chapter you should:
- Know the three areas of a Projektdokumentation (project documentation) and their weighting
- Be able to create a sensible outline
- Avoid the most common mistakes

---

## Core Content

The documentation presents the **complete project work in approx. 15 pages**. The art lies in the **balance between necessary information and a concise, precise presentation** – the number of pages must reflect the actual course of the project (not 10 pages of the project idea and then hardly any implementation).

### The three areas (project time)

| Area | Share |
|------|-------|
| **Planning** | ~25 % |
| **Execution** | ~60 % |
| **Reflection** | ~15 % |

> The time for the documentation (usually **8–10 hrs**) is included in the **80 project hours**.

### Possible outline

**Planning**
- Introduction of the company and yourself (area of deployment, focus)
- Introduction of the customer, project description (project environment)
- Communication/interfaces to other departments
- Approach (software development model)
- Business aspects (cost analysis)
- Time planning (as in the proposal), IST analysis (actual-state analysis) / SOLL concept (target-state concept)

**Execution**
- Implementation of the SOLL concept (described in a process-oriented way)
- Milestones, problems encountered and how they were resolved
- Excerpts from the source code, important diagrams (ER diagram, UML)
- Quality assurance measures (tests)

**Reflection**
- Soll-Ist-Vergleich (target/actual comparison) (time, costs, goals)
- Lessons learned, outlook

---

## Key Terms

| Term | Explanation |
|------|-------------|
| **Process-oriented report** | Description along the course of the project (not just the result) |
| **IST analysis / SOLL concept** | Initial situation vs. desired state |
| **Soll-Ist-Vergleich (target/actual comparison)** | Comparison of planning and actual result in the reflection |

---

## Exam Tips

Common mistakes (from preparation sources, "17 most common mistakes"):

- **Unbalanced page distribution** (too much planning, too little execution)
- Execution described only in a **result-oriented** rather than **process-oriented** way
- **Reflection missing** or remaining superficial (no real target/actual comparison)
- No **business analysis** (cost/benefit, amortization)
- Too many code listings instead of **relevant excerpts**
- Spelling/structure → have it proofread

## Practice Exercise

**Task:** Create an outline for an AE project with a realistic page distribution across 15 pages (planning/execution/reflection in a ratio of ~25/60/15).

<details>
<summary>Solution note</summary>

For 15 pages, roughly: ~4 pages of planning, ~9 pages of execution, ~2 pages of reflection (plus cover page/lists of contents outside the count).

</details>
