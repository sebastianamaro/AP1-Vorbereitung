# The Projektantrag (Project Proposal)

## Learning Objectives

After this chapter you should:
- Know which contents a Projektantrag (project proposal) must mandatorily include
- Avoid the most common mistakes in the proposal
- Understand what happens if the proposal is returned or rejected

---

## Core Content

The Projektantrag is usually submitted **online via the IHK platform**. It informs the **Prüfungsausschuss (examination board)** about the planned project and must give it a **complete picture** – not every detail, but everything essential.

### Mandatory content

- **Project description** with the underlying **Geschäftsprozess (business process)** or the problem – precisely formulated
- **Schnittstellen (interfaces)**: technical *and* personal (e.g. project contact person)
- **Customer benefit** of the project
- **Project phases** with **time estimates**
- **Planned documentation**: process-oriented project report as well as further documents (e.g. Pflichtenheft, test documentation)

### Process & approval

- **Returned for revision:** usually only missing details – calmly add them and resubmit. No reason to panic.
- **Rejection:** the board has identified serious deficiencies (e.g. the scope is inappropriate in terms of time, or the topic does not fit the training occupation). In that case, only a **new project** helps.

---

## Key Terms

| Term | Explanation |
|------|-------------|
| **Prüfungsausschuss (examination board)** | IHK panel that evaluates the proposal, documentation and presentation |
| **Geschäftsprozess (business process)** | The operational workflow that the project supports/improves |
| **Pflichtenheft (functional specification)** | Implementation of the requirements drawn up by the contractor (How?) |
| **Schnittstellen (interfaces)** | Technical and personal points of contact of the project |

---

## Exam Tips

The Projektantrag is the **business card** – an incomplete proposal makes a poor first impression. Common mistakes (from preparation sources, "14 most common mistakes"):

- Geschäftsprozess/problem described too vaguely
- Customer benefit missing or remaining unclear
- Project scope unrealistic in terms of time (well below/above 80 hrs)
- Schnittstellen not mentioned
- Spelling mistakes → **definitely have it proofread**
- Write **at least a few sentences** for every required aspect
- Have the proposal read by a **knowledgeable person unfamiliar with the project** (reveals logical errors)
