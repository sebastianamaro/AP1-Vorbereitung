# UX / Usability

## Learning Objectives

After this chapter you should:
- Distinguish User Experience (UX) from usability
- Know the three usability criteria of ISO 9241-11

---

## Core Content

### Usability vs. UX

| Term | Meaning |
|---------|-----------|
| **Usability (Gebrauchstauglichkeit)** | How **well** can a product be used for a task? (Focus: use) |
| **User Experience (UX)** | The **entire experience** before, during and after use (Focus: experience, emotion, expectation) |

> **Usability is a part of UX.** UX is more comprehensive (incl. brand, aesthetics, emotions, the entire "journey").

### Usability according to ISO 9241-11

Usability = usability within the context of use, measured by three criteria:

| Criterion | Meaning |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Effectiveness** | the goal is achieved completely and correctly |
| **Efficiency** | with reasonable effort (time/resources) |
| **Satisfaction** | positive attitude/acceptance by the users |

### Methods

- **Usability test** (observe users while they work on tasks)
- **Heuristic evaluation** (expert check against principles)
- **A/B test**, user surveys, prototyping

---

## Key Terms

| Term | Explanation |
|---------|-----------|
| **Usability** | Fitness for use (effectiveness, efficiency, satisfaction) |
| **UX** | Overall experience of use (includes usability) |
| **ISO 9241-11** | Standard for the definition of usability |
| **Usability test** | Observing users while they work on tasks |

---

## Exam Tips

- **Usability ⊂ UX** – usability is part of the more comprehensive UX.
- Memorize the **three ISO 9241-11 criteria** (effectiveness, efficiency, satisfaction).
- Make the connection to software ergonomics (ISO 9241) and to GUI design.
