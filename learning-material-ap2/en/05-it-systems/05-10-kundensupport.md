# Customer Support

## Learning Objectives

After this chapter you should be able to:
- Distinguish support levels 1–3 and their tasks
- Understand the escalation principle
- Know the basic terms of support (ticket, SLA)

---

## Core Content

### Support Level Structure

| Level | Tasks / Characteristics |
|-------|-------------------------|
| **Level-1-Support** (first-level support) | First point of contact; takes in requests, solves simple/known cases using **documented procedures** (FAQ, knowledge base); qualifies and forwards |
| **Level-2-Support** (second-level support) | **First escalation stage**; deeper technical analysis, more complex problems |
| **Level-3-Support** (third-level support) | Highest stage; specialists/developers/manufacturer; fundamental, rare or new problems |

**Escalation:** If one level cannot solve a problem, it is **forwarded** (escalated) to the next higher level. The goal is to solve as much as possible already at level 1 (cost/time).

### Further Terms

- **Ticket / ticketing system:** records and tracks every request (traceability)
- **SLA (Service Level Agreement):** agreed service quality (e.g. response/resolution times)
- **First-level resolution rate:** the share of cases already solved at level 1
- **Knowledge base:** documented solution paths

---

## Key Terms

| Term | Explanation |
|------|-------------|
| **Level 1/2/3** | Support stages from simple to specialized |
| **Escalation** | Forwarding to the next higher stage |
| **SLA** | Agreed service levels (response/resolution times) |
| **Ticket** | A recorded, traceable support request |

---

## Exam Tips

- Assign **level 1/2/3** and the **escalation direction** (1 → 2 → 3) confidently.
- Level 1 = first point of contact + documented standard solutions; level 2 = **first escalation stage**.
- Name SLA and the ticketing system as support fundamentals.
