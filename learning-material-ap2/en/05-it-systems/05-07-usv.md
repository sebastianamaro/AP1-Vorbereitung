# Uninterruptible Power Supply (USV)

## Learning Objectives

After this chapter you should be able to:
- Know the purpose of a USV (Unterbrechungsfreie Stromversorgung / uninterruptible power supply)
- Distinguish the three USV classes (VFD/VI/VFI)
- Select a suitable USV for a scenario

---

## Core Content

### Purpose

A **USV** (Unterbrechungsfreie Stromversorgung / uninterruptible power supply) bridges power problems with **buffer batteries** and keeps critical systems (servers, network) running temporarily. Many USVs send a **signal for an orderly shutdown** to connected servers when the charge level is low.

### The Three USV Classes

| Class | Also called | Switchover time | Protection | Use |
|-------|-------------|-----------------|------------|-----|
| **VFD** (Voltage Frequency Dependent) | **Offline/Standby** | short switchover time | outage (rough) | single PCs, home office |
| **VI** (Voltage Independent) | **Line-Interactive** | short switchover time | outage + voltage fluctuations (AVR) | small servers/networks |
| **VFI** (Voltage Frequency Independent) | **Online / double conversion** | **uninterrupted** (0 ms) | comprehensive (outage, fluctuations, frequency) | data centers, critical systems |

- **Online USV (VFI):** power runs **permanently** through the inverter → no switchover time, best protection, but more expensive.
- **Offline USV (VFD):** the battery only kicks in **on outage** (a short switchover gap) → cheap.

---

## Key Terms

| Term | Explanation |
|------|-------------|
| **USV** | Battery-backed bridging of power problems |
| **Offline/VFD** | Battery only on outage, cheap |
| **Line-Interactive/VI** | additionally voltage regulation (AVR) |
| **Online/VFI** | double conversion, uninterrupted, best protection |

---

## Exam Tips

- Know the **three classes** and their protection level/switchover behavior.
- **Online (VFI) = no switchover moment**, for data centers; **Offline (VFD)** for single PCs.
- Classify the USV as a measure for the protection goal **availability**.
