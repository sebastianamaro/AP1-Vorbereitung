# Project Management – Fundamentals

## Learning Objectives

After this chapter you should be able to:
- Explain the **Magisches Dreieck** (magic triangle) of project management
- Distinguish important PM standards from unrelated terms
- Classify organizational models, organizational structures and methods/instruments

---

## Core Content

### The Magisches Dreieck (magic triangle)

Every project operates within the tension of three competing target dimensions:

```
              Zeit
              /  \
             /    \
            /      \
       Kosten ──── Leistung
       (Budget)    (Qualität/Umfang)
```

If one dimension changes, it affects the others: if, for example, the **time** is shortened, the **costs** usually rise or the **performance/quality** drops. The goal of project management is a sustainable balance. (Often **quality** is additionally named as a fourth dimension at the center.)

### PM Standards and Methods

Project management includes, among others:

- **DIN 69901** – German standard for project management
- **PMBOK** – Project Management Body of Knowledge (PMI)
- **IPMA** – International Project Management Association
- **PRINCE2** – process-oriented PM method

> **Not** part of it (typical distractors in exercises): **SOAP, REST, ISAPI** (web/interface technology), **DIN EN ISO 9241** (software ergonomics).

### Organization

| Category | Examples |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Organizational models** | Line organization, matrix organization, autonomous (pure) project organization |
| **Organizational structures** | Project management, project team, steering committee (Lenkungsausschuss) |
| **Methods / instruments** | Netzplan (network diagram), Gantt chart, milestones, risk analysis |

- **Pure project organization:** the team is completely detached from the line – fast, but resource-intensive.
- **Matrix organization:** employees report functionally to the line and project-wise to the project management – flexible, but with conflict potential (dual leadership).
- **Line organization (influence PM):** project management only coordinates, no authority to issue directives.

## Key Terms

| Term | Explanation |
|---------|-----------|
| **Magisches Dreieck** (magic triangle) | Goal conflict between time, cost and performance/quality |
| **Meilenstein** (milestone) | Important intermediate event with a result (duration 0) |
| **Lenkungsausschuss** (steering committee) | Superior decision-making body of the project |
| **Matrix organization** | Dual assignment: line + project |

---

## Exam Tips

- The **Magisches Dreieck** (magic triangle) is a perennial favorite – be able to explain it, including the interdependencies.
- **Matching tasks** are common: separate genuine PM standards (DIN 69901, PMBOK, IPMA, PRINCE2) from web/other terms (SOAP, REST, ISAPI).
- Have **one advantage and one disadvantage** ready for each form of organization.
