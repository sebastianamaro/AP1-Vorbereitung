# SCRUM and KANBAN

## Learning Objectives

After this chapter you should be able to:
- Know the roles, artifacts and events of Scrum
- Understand the principles of Kanban (board, WIP limits)
- Recognize and correct common false statements about Scrum/Kanban

---

## Core Content

### Scrum

Agile framework that delivers iteratively in **sprints** (usually 1–4 weeks).

**Roles**
- **Product Owner** – responsible for the product backlog and the value of the product
- **Scrum Master** – ensures the process, removes impediments (not a "boss")
- **Development team** – self-organized, small (recommended ~3–9 people)

**Artifacts**
- **Product Backlog** – prioritized overall list of all requirements
- **Sprint Backlog** – items selected for the sprint
- **Increment** – runnable result at the end of the sprint

**Events**
- **Sprint Planning** – selection and planning of the sprint content
- **Daily Scrum** – short daily standup (~15 min.)
- **Sprint Review** – presentation of the increment
- **Sprint Retrospective** – process improvement

### Kanban

Method for **controlling and visualizing the workflow**.

- **Kanban board** (whiteboard/tool) visualizes the work process in columns (e.g. *To Do → In Progress → Done*)
- **WIP limits** (Work in Progress) limit the parallel tasks per station → reveals **bottlenecks** and minimizes them
- The goal is an **even, continuous flow**, no fixed iterations
- **Teamwork**: identify and solve problems within the team as early as possible

### Common False Statements (Careful, exam trap!)

| Statement | Correct? |
|---------|----------|
| "Scrum and Kanban are mutually exclusive." | ❌ False – they can be combined (**Scrumban**) |
| "Kanban reveals bottlenecks and helps to minimize them." | ✅ Correct |
| "Each station should process **as many** tickets as possible." | ❌ False – **limit WIP**, do not maximize |
| "Scrum is mainly for very large teams (>100)." | ❌ False – small teams |

## Key Terms

| Term | Explanation |
|---------|-----------|
| **Sprint** | Fixed period at the end of which there is an increment |
| **Product/Sprint Backlog** | Overall or sprint task list |
| **WIP limit** | Upper limit of parallel tasks per column (Kanban) |
| **Scrumban** | Combination of Scrum and Kanban |

## Exam Tips

- Be able to confidently name the four **Scrum events** and three **roles**.
- **Kanban core:** visualize the flow + **limit WIP** (do not maximize!) – a popular mistake in exercises.
- Typical task (as in the book): **check notes/statements for correctness** – cover the false-statements table above.

## Practice Exercise

**Task (based on ConSystem GmbH):** Check the colleagues' notes for correctness and correct them (e.g. "Scrum and Kanban are mutually exclusive", "each station should process as many tickets as possible", "Scrum is suitable for teams >100").

<details>
<summary>Solution</summary>

All three statements mentioned are **false**: Scrum and Kanban can be combined; Kanban **limits** parallel work (WIP) instead of maximizing it; Scrum is intended for **small** teams. The statement about bottlenecks and the importance of teamwork is correct.

</details>
