# SMART Goals, Projektstrukturplan & Team Phases

## Learning Objectives

After this chapter you should be able to:
- Formulate goals according to SMART
- Classify a Projektstrukturplan (work breakdown structure, PSP)
- Know the team phases according to Tuckman

---

## Core Content

### SMART Goals

Good goals are **SMART**:

| Letter | Meaning |
|-----------|-----------|
| **S**pecific | formulated concretely/unambiguously |
| **M**easurable | with a verifiable criterion |
| **A**ttractive (Accepted) | desirable/accepted |
| **R**ealistic | achievable |
| **T**ime-bound | with a clear deadline |

*Example:* "Reduce the loading time of the homepage from 4 s to < 2 s by 30 June."

### Projektstrukturplan (work breakdown structure, PSP)

The **PSP** breaks the project down hierarchically into **subtasks/work packages** ("What has to be done?"). It is the **basis** for scheduling/network planning and effort estimation.

```
Projekt
├── Teilprojekt A
│   ├── Arbeitspaket A1
│   └── Arbeitspaket A2
└── Teilprojekt B
    └── Arbeitspaket B1
```

> **PSP vs. Netzplan (network diagram):** PSP = *structure* (**what**), Netzplan = *sequence/dependencies* (**when**).

### Team Phases according to Tuckman

| Phase | Characteristic |
|-------|-------------|
| **Forming** | orientation, getting to know each other |
| **Storming** | conflicts, clarification of roles |
| **Norming** | establishing rules/collaboration |
| **Performing** | high-performing collaboration |
| *(Adjourning)* | dissolution after the end of the project |

---

## Key Terms

| Term | Explanation |
|---------|-----------|
| **SMART** | Specific, Measurable, Attractive, Realistic, Time-bound |
| **PSP** (Projektstrukturplan / work breakdown structure) | Hierarchical breakdown into work packages (what) |
| **Tuckman phases** | Forming, Storming, Norming, Performing |

---

## Exam Tips

- Be able to spell out **SMART** and formulate a SMART goal.
- Distinguish **PSP (what) vs. Netzplan (when)**.
- Tuckman phases in the **correct order**.
