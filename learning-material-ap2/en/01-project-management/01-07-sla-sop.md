# SLA & SOP

## Learning Objectives

After this chapter you should be able to:
- Know a Service Level Agreement (SLA) and its contents
- Classify Standard Operating Procedures (SOP)
- Distinguish SLA/SOP from Lastenheft/Pflichtenheft

---

## Core Content

### SLA – Service Level Agreement

An **SLA** is a **contractual agreement about the service quality** between service provider and customer. Typical contents:

- **Availability** (e.g. 99.9 % → approx. 8.7 h downtime/year)
- **Response and recovery times** (per priority)
- **Escalation paths** and contact persons
- **Measurement methods** and reporting
- **Consequences** of non-compliance (e.g. contractual penalties/credits)

### SOP – Standard Operating Procedure

An **SOP** is a **standardized work instruction** for recurring processes – reproducible, traceable, auditable (e.g. "procedure in the event of a system failure", "onboarding of new users").

### Distinguishing the Document Types

| Document | Target group | Content |
|----------|-----------|--------|
| **Lastenheft** (requirements specification by the client) | client | *what* is required |
| **Pflichtenheft** (functional specification by the contractor) | contractor | *how* it is implemented |
| **SLA** | client ⇄ contractor (contract) | service quality/metrics |
| **SOP** | operations/employees | standardized work instruction |

---

## Key Terms

| Term | Explanation |
|---------|-----------|
| **SLA** | Agreed service quality (availability, times) |
| **Availability** | Share of failure-free time (e.g. 99.9 %) |
| **SOP** | Standardized, reproducible work instruction |

---

## Exam Tips

- Name SLA contents (availability, response times, escalation, consequences).
- Distinguish **SLA (contract/metrics) vs. SOP (work instruction)**.
- Correctly assign the four documents (Lastenheft/Pflichtenheft/SLA/SOP).
