# Stakeholder and Risk Analysis

## Learning Objectives

After this chapter you should be able to:
- Identify internal and external Stakeholder (interested parties)
- Apply the Stakeholder matrix (influence/interest) and derive strategies
- Carry out a risk analysis with probability of occurrence and impact

---

## Core Content

### Stakeholder Analysis

**Stakeholder** are all persons/groups who have an interest in the project or are affected by it.

- **Internal:** IT department, HR department, marketing, management, **Betriebsrat** (works council), project team
- **External:** customers, suppliers, **external partner agencies**, authorities

#### Stakeholder Matrix

Stakeholder are classified by **influence (power)** and **interest**:

```
 Einfluss
   hoch │  zufriedenstellen   │   eng einbinden
        │  (z. B. Betriebsrat)│   (z. B. Auftraggeber)
        ├─────────────────────┼──────────────────────
   niedr│  beobachten         │   informieren
        │                     │   (z. B. Endnutzer)
        └─────────────────────┴──────────────────────
                niedrig               hoch   Interesse
```

| Quadrant | Strategy |
|------|-----------|
| high influence / high interest | **engage closely / actively manage** |
| high influence / low interest | **keep satisfied** |
| low influence / high interest | **keep informed** |
| low influence / low interest | **monitor** |

### Risk Analysis

Risks are assessed by **probability of occurrence** and **extent of damage (impact)**:

**Risk value = probability of occurrence × impact**

Strategies for dealing with risks:
- **Avoid** (eliminate the cause)
- **Reduce** (lower probability/impact)
- **Transfer** (e.g. insurance, outsourcing)
- **Accept** (deliberately bear it, possibly build a reserve)

---

## Key Terms

| Term | Explanation |
|---------|-----------|
| **Stakeholder** | Person/group with interest in or affected by the project |
| **Stakeholder matrix** | Classification by influence and interest |
| **Risk value** | Probability of occurrence × impact |
| **Risk strategies** | Avoid, reduce, transfer, accept |

---

## Exam Tips

- Assign the **matrix axes** (influence/power ↔ interest) and the **four strategies** confidently.
- On "internal vs. external": the **Betriebsrat** (works council) is *internal*, the **external agency** is *external* – a frequent classification detail.
- Always assess risks with **both** dimensions (probability *and* impact).

## Practice Exercise

**Task (based on ConSystem GmbH):** A customer (350 employees) introduces an intranet information system in 4 months; affected are IT, HR, marketing, Betriebsrat (works council); the design is handled by an external agency. Name two internal and two external Stakeholder each and place them in the matrix.

<details>
<summary>Solution hint</summary>

- **Internal:** IT department, Betriebsrat (works council) (high influence → keep satisfied/engage)
- **External:** external design agency, customer/client (high interest → keep informed/engage)

</details>
