# Sustainability in the Training Company

## Learning Objectives

After this chapter you should be able to:
- Explain Nachhaltigkeit (sustainability) and its three dimensions
- Classify the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)
- Name concrete measures in IT operations

---

## Core Content

### Concept & three dimensions

**Nachhaltigkeit** (sustainability) means meeting the needs of the present **without** jeopardising the opportunities of future generations. It rests on **three dimensions** (the sustainability triangle):

| Dimension | Focus | Example in the company |
|-----------|-------|---------------------|
| **Ecological** | Environment/resources | Green IT, saving electricity, recycling of old devices |
| **Economic** | economic viability | durable investments, efficient processes |
| **Social** | people/society | fair working conditions, further training, health |

Action is only sustainable in the long term when all three are in balance.

### Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)

The **17 Sustainable Development Goals** of the UN (Agenda 2030) form the global framework (e.g. affordable/clean energy, decent work, responsible consumption).

### Green IT – concrete measures

- **Energy-efficient hardware**, server virtualisation/consolidation
- **Green electricity** (Ökostrom), efficient cooling in the data centre (PUE)
- **Extended service life** of devices (see the catalogue: "use devices beyond the depreciation period")
- **Proper recycling** / take-back of old devices
- **Resource-efficient software development** (efficient code)

## Key Terms

| Term | Explanation |
|---------|-----------|
| **Three dimensions** | Ecological, economic, social |
| **SDGs** | 17 UN Sustainable Development Goals (Agenda 2030) |
| **Green IT** | Environmentally friendly design/use of IT |
| **Nachhaltigkeitsdreieck** (sustainability triangle) | Balance of the three dimensions |
