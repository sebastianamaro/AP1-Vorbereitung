# Legal Regulations in the Company and in Customer Relationships

## Learning Objectives

After this chapter you should be able to:
- Know the types of contract and how a contract comes into being
- Classify the sales contract, AGB (standard terms and conditions) and Gewährleistung (statutory warranty)
- Distinguish customer loyalty from contractual commitment

---

## Core Content

### Contract & how it comes into being

A **contract** comes into being through two matching declarations of intent: an **offer** and an **acceptance** (BGB – German Civil Code). Important types of contract in the IT context:

| Contract | Content |
|---------|--------|
| **Kaufvertrag** (sales contract) | Transfer of ownership of an item for money (§ 433 BGB) |
| **Werkvertrag** (contract for work/results) | What is owed is a **result/work** (e.g. finished software) |
| **Dienstvertrag** (service contract) | What is owed is the **activity** (e.g. consulting/support) |

> For **software projects** the **Werkvertrag** (contract for a specific result) is usually the relevant one (result = a working product).

### AGB & Gewährleistung

- **AGB** (Allgemeine Geschäftsbedingungen – standard business terms and conditions): pre-formulated contract terms; subject to content review (no surprising/unreasonable clauses).
- **Gewährleistung** (statutory warranty / liability for defects): a legal claim in the event of defects (subsequent performance, price reduction, withdrawal, damages) – usually **2 years**.
- **Garantie** (guarantee): a voluntary, additional assurance by the manufacturer/seller.

### Customer loyalty

**Customer loyalty** (Kundenbindung) does not just mean a **contractual** commitment, but above all an **emotional attachment** built on **satisfaction** and **mutual trust** → repeat purchases, recommendations.

## Key Terms

| Term | Explanation |
|---------|-----------|
| **Werkvertrag** | Owes a result (finished work) |
| **Dienstvertrag** | Owes an activity |
| **Gewährleistung** | Statutory liability for defects (usually 2 years) |
| **Garantie** | Voluntary assurance |

---

## Exam Tips

- **Werkvertrag (result) vs. Dienstvertrag (activity)** – an important distinction for IT projects.
- Do not confuse **Gewährleistung (statutory) vs. Garantie (voluntary)**.
- Contract = offer + acceptance.
