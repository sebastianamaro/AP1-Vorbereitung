# Definitions (Art. 4 DSGVO)

## Learning Objectives

After this chapter you should be able to:
- Correctly assign the central DSGVO concepts
- Distinguish controller, processor, recipient and third party
- Know the special categories of personal data

---

## Core Content

### Central concepts (Art. 4)

| Term | Explanation |
|---------|-----------|
| **Personal data** | Information about an identified/identifiable natural person |
| **Processing** | Any operation on data (collecting, storing, modifying, deleting …) |
| **Controller** | Body that decides on the purposes and means of the processing |
| **Processor** | Processes data **on behalf of** the controller |
| **Recipient** | Body to which data is disclosed (also internally) |
| **Third party** | Person/body outside the controller, processor and the directly authorized persons |
| **Consent** | Freely given, specific, informed and unambiguous indication of the data subject's wishes |
| **Pseudonymization** | Processing such that data can no longer be attributed to a person without additional information (kept separately) |

### Special categories (Art. 9)

Especially worthy of protection – processing generally prohibited (except for exemptions): among others **health data**, **genetic data**, biometric data, data on ethnic origin, political/religious beliefs, trade union membership, sex life.

### Pseudonymization vs. anonymization

- **Pseudonymization:** re-identification **possible** with additional information → remains personal (DSGVO applies).
- **Anonymization:** re-identification **no longer** possible → no personal reference (DSGVO does not apply).

---

## Key Terms

| Term | Explanation |
|---------|-----------|
| **Controller vs. processor** | Decides on purpose/means vs. processes on behalf |
| **Recipient vs. third party** | To whom data is disclosed vs. outsiders |
| **Pseudonymization** | Separation of additional information, reversible |
| **Special categories** | Health, genetic, biometric data etc. (Art. 9) |

---

## Exam Tips

- **Controller vs. processor** and **recipient vs. third party** are classic assignment traps.
- **Pseudonymization ≠ anonymization** – only anonymization removes the data from the DSGVO.
- **Consent** must be *freely given, informed, unambiguous*.
