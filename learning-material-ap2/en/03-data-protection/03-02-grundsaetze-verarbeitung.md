# Principles of Processing (Art. 5 DSGVO)

## Learning Objectives

After this chapter you should be able to:
- Know the principles for processing personal data (Art. 5 DSGVO – EU General Data Protection Regulation)
- Be able to check planned data processing operations for DSGVO compliance

---

## Core Content

### The principles (Art. 5 para. 1)

| Principle | Meaning |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Lawfulness, fairness, transparency** | Processing on a valid legal basis and comprehensible to the person |
| **Zweckbindung (purpose limitation)** | Collection only for specified, explicit, legitimate purposes; no incompatible further processing |
| **Datenminimierung (data minimization)** | Only data that is adequate for the purpose and limited to what is necessary |
| **Accuracy** | Data factually correct and up to date; delete/correct inaccurate data |
| **Storage limitation** | Identifiable storage only for as long as necessary |
| **Integrity and confidentiality** | Protection through appropriate technical/organizational measures |

In addition (Art. 5 para. 2) there is the **accountability** principle: the controller must be able to **demonstrate** compliance with these principles.

### Application: checking compliance

When checking a planned processing operation (e.g. a contact form), you go through the principles – especially **Datenminimierung (data minimization)** and **Zweckbindung (purpose limitation)**:

> **Example:** A contact form collects surname, first name, email, phone, **number of employees** and **year of founding**. For pure contact purposes, the employee count and year of founding are **not required** → **violation of Datenminimierung (data minimization)**. Admissible would be the fields needed to make contact (marking mandatory vs. optional fields where appropriate).

---

## Key Terms

| Term | Explanation |
|---------|-----------|
| **Zweckbindung** | Use data only for the specified purpose |
| **Datenminimierung** | As little data as possible, as much as necessary |
| **Storage limitation** | Deletion as soon as the purpose is fulfilled |
| **Accountability** | The controller's duty to provide evidence |

---

## Exam Tips

- Remember the **six principles** (para. 1) plus **accountability** (para. 2).
- Typical task: assess a planned processing operation – usually **Datenminimierung (data minimization)** is the crux (unnecessary fields).
- Always justify **with the specific principle** (not just "that's not allowed").

## Practice Exercise

**Task (based on ConSystem GmbH):** Using Art. 5 DSGVO, check whether a contact form with the fields surname, first name, email, phone, employee count, year of founding is data-protection compliant.

<details>
<summary>Solution hint</summary>

For pure contact purposes, the employee count and year of founding violate **Datenminimierung (data minimization)**; they are not required for the purpose.

</details>
