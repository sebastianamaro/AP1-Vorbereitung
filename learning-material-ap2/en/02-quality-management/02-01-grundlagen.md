# Quality Management – Fundamentals

## Learning Objectives

After this chapter you should be able to:
- Distinguish between Kaizen, Lean Management and Total Quality Management
- Explain Six Sigma, DMAIC and DPMO
- Follow the reasoning behind the 3.4-DPMO requirement

---

## Core Content

### QM Philosophies

| Concept | Core idea |
|---------|----------|
| **Kaizen** | Continuous improvement in small steps (Japanese for "change for the better"); the basis of the CIP |
| **Lean Management** | Avoid waste (Japanese *Muda*), maximize value for the customer, lean processes |
| **Total Quality Management (TQM)** | Holistic approach: quality as the task of **all** employees and processes, customer-oriented |

### Six Sigma

A data-driven QM method with the goal of nearly defect-free processes.

- **DMAIC** – improvement cycle for process improvement: **D**efine, **M**easure, **A**nalyze, **I**mprove, **C**ontrol
- **The name "Six Sigma":** from statistics – the process should be so stable that **six standard deviations (6σ)** fit between the mean and the tolerance limit.
- **DPMO** – *Defects Per Million Opportunities*.

#### Numerical verification

99.99966 % defect-free ⇒ defect rate = 100 % − 99.99966 % = **0.00034 %** = 0.0000034.
0.0000034 × 1,000,000 = **3.4 DPMO**. ⇒ The two requirements **match**.

---

## Key Terms

| Term | Explanation |
|---------|-----------|
| **Kaizen / CIP** | Continuous improvement process in small steps |
| **DMAIC** | Define–Measure–Analyze–Improve–Control (Six Sigma cycle) |
| **DPMO** | Defects per Million Opportunities |
| **TQM** | Company-wide, customer-oriented quality management |

---

## Exam Tips

- Know **DMAIC** by heart (the order matters!).
- The **3.4 DPMO ↔ 99.99966 %** calculation is a typical "prove this numerically" task.
- Clearly distinguish Kaizen (small steps) vs. TQM (holistic) vs. Lean (avoiding waste).
