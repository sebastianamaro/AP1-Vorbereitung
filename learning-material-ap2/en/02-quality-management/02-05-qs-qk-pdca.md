# QA vs. QC, PDCA & Target-Actual Comparison

## Learning Objectives

After this chapter you should be able to:
- Distinguish quality assurance and quality control
- Explain the PDCA cycle and CIP
- Calculate a deviation via a target-actual comparison

---

## Core Content

### Quality assurance (QA) vs. quality control (QC)

| | Quality assurance (QA) | Quality control (QC) |
|--|-------------------------|-------------------------|
| **Goal** | **prevent** defects | **find** defects |
| **Timing** | **proactive** (beforehand/accompanying) | **reactive** (checking the result) |
| **Examples** | processes, standards, reviews, training | tests, measurements, acceptance inspection |

> Mnemonic: **QA = proactive (prevent)**, **QC = reactive (find)**.

### PDCA cycle & CIP

The **PDCA cycle** (Deming) is the basis of the **continuous improvement process (CIP)**:

```
Plan  → Do → Check → Act → (Plan again …)
plan    do    check  improve
```

- **Plan:** plan the goal/measure · **Do:** implement (small/trial) · **Check:** verify the result · **Act:** standardize if successful, otherwise adjust.

### Target-actual comparison

Deviation between plan and reality (e.g. time, costs):

**Deviation (%) = ((Actual − Target) / Target) × 100**

*Example:* planned 20 h, actual 24 h → (24−20)/20 × 100 = **+20 %**.

---

## Key Terms

| Term | Explanation |
|---------|-----------|
| **QA / QC** | Prevent defects (proactive) / find defects (reactive) |
| **PDCA** | Plan-Do-Check-Act |
| **CIP** | Continuous improvement process |
| **Target-actual comparison** | Deviation between plan and reality |

---

## Exam Tips

- **QA vs. QC** (proactive/reactive) – classic distinction.
- **PDCA** in the correct order; the basis of the CIP.
- Be able to apply the **target-actual formula** (percentage deviation).

## Practice Exercise

**Task:** (1) Assign "code review", "acceptance test", "training", "measurement" to QA or QC. (2) A project was planned for 50 h but took 60 h – calculate the deviation.

<details>
<summary>Solution</summary>

QA: code review, training; QC: acceptance test, measurement. Deviation: (60−50)/50×100 = **+20 %**.

</details>
